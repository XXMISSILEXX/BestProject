OLED display with HDR

While all three new iPhone models support HDR10 and Dolby Vision content playback, only the iPhone X comes with a true HDR display. This is achieved by using OLED technology instead of LCD with IPS background found on iPhone 8 and 8 Plus. The OLED screen on the iPhone X has a significantly higher contrast ratio, 1,000,000:1 compared to 1,300:1 on the iPhone 8 Plus and 1,400:1 on the iPhone 8.

The iPhone X's OLED display, called Super Retina HD, is able to produce deeper blacks by turning off the black pixels, while with the LCD it's still on. Thanks to that, the battery on the OLED screen has better usage time.

Longer battery life

As usual, Apple does not detail the performance of the battery on the new iPhone series, but the company says that the iPhone X is capable of lasting more than 2 hours of use compared to the iPhone 7 despite it being equipped with all the features. all new technologies.

It's not clear what Apple's reference to 2 hours is, but the company provided a comparison chart showing that the iPhone X and 8 Plus can last around 21 hours of talk time, while the iPhone 8 is 14 hours. For internet use, the iPhone 8 Plus can reach 13 hours, while the iPhone X and 8 are 12 hours. When playing video, the iPhone 8 and X provide about 12 hours, and the iPhone 8 Plus 13 hours. But when playing music, the iPhone X and 8 Plus lasted up to 60 hours, higher than the 40 hours of the iPhone 8.

Dual Optical Image Stabilization (OIS)

Although all three iPhone members have 12MP rear and 7MP front cameras, both the iPhone X and 8 Plus add a second camera sensor on the back. These dual cameras have 2 different lenses, wide angle and telephoto, but Apple has improved it on the iPhone X.

Face ID

The iPhone X comes with a TrueDepth camera to support the Face ID function for face unlock instead of Touch ID due to the nearly full-screen design on the front. Face ID uses an infrared camera and other sensors to build and read a map of the face as it scans.

The accuracy of Face ID is advertised by Apple as superior even when compared to Touch ID, which is also used for mobile payment authentication. This feature is very useful when you read a newspaper page but are bothered when the screen is locked. Only iPhone X is equipped with Face ID, while iPhone 8 and 8 Plus only support fingerprint scanning with Touch ID.

Take portraits with the front camera

The iPhone X's front camera comes with some skills that the iPhone 8 and 8 Plus can't do. Thanks to the addition of a sensor on the front, the TrueDepth camera on the iPhone X can take photos in portrait mode, which the iPhone 7 Plus can do but only with the dual cameras on the back.
